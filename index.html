Security

The home alarm is operated by the JVA remote fob on the key ring.

  • 1 chirp = armed  |  2 chirps = disarmed
  • Please arm the system when you leave the house for an extended time
  • Indoor cameras only power on when the alarm is armed. They are never on while you are home.
  • Two front cameras run 24/7 and record on motion

Please message us when you leave at the end of your stay.

Guest responsibilities

During your stay

A few things need attention while you are here. Thank you in advance.

Bins

Put bins on the footpath on Monday night. Pickup is Tuesday morning. Bring them back by Tuesday evening.

  • Red lid — general rubbish. Out every week.
  • Yellow lid — recycling. Alternates with green bin.
  • Green lid — organics. Alternates with yellow bin.

Recycling: plastic, glass, cardboard. No soft plastics. If unsure, use the red bin.

Watering Plants

This is important — please do not skip.

  • Citrus in pots (back wall) — every 2–3 days.
  • Other pots (front and back) — once a week, depending on weather.
  • Indoor plants — do not overwater.

Hoses at each side of the house and front right. Watering can under the BBQ.

Pool Care

  • Keep water level above the black tile line around the top of the pool
  • Add 1 chlorine tablet to the floating holder every 2 days (more in hot weather, less in cold)
  • Tablets and test strips are in the pool pump shed (behind the orange wall)
  • Pool fence gates must be closed and latched at all times — this is a legal requirement

In winter the pool may be "sleeping" — in which case it needs no maintenance — ask us if you are unsure.

Composting

Coffee grounds, fruit and vegetable scraps can be composted. No fish, meat, bones, or bread.

  • Temporary container is by the kitchen sink
  • Empty into the tumbling compost bin in the garden on the western side
  • Turn the bin each time you add to it

Mail

The letterbox key is inside the pantry door. Collect mail and place it in the wooden in-tray on the desk in the music room.

Explore the region

Local area

There is much to enjoy within easy reach of the house.

Day Trips

  • Dayboro — 20 min. Country cafés and markets. Avoid Monday.
  • Maleny — rainforest walks, Maleny Botanic Gardens, platypus spotting on Obi Obi Creek
  • Montville — boutique town, The Edge restaurant with hinterland views
  • Bribie Island — Woorim surf beach or calm water at Bongaree
  • Redcliffe — 13 km coastal path, Bee Gees Way, Sunday markets, jetty
  • Australia Zoo — Beerwah (koalas, crocs, wombats and more)
  • Glasshouse Mountains — walks and climbs
  • Mount Mee / D'Aguilar NP — walks, cycling trails, Glass House Mountain views
  • Brisbane City — South Bank, GOMA, Queensland Museum, Queen Street Mall, Botanical Gardens
  • Sunshine Coast — Caloundra to Noosa

  • Pool table: balls, cues, and chalk provided.
